Nutrition

Macronutrients:

Carbohydrates:

  • Make complex carbohydrates, such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables, a primary source of energy.
  • Limit refined carbohydrates and added sugars.

Proteins:

  • Include lean protein sources in your diet, such as poultry, fish, beans, legumes, tofu, and low-fat dairy.
  • Balance protein intake throughout the day to support muscle maintenance and repair.

Fats:

  • Choose healthy fats, including sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil, and fatty fish.
  • Limit saturated and trans fats found in processed and fried foods.

Micronutrients:

Vitamins and Minerals:

  • Consume a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables to ensure a broad range of vitamins and minerals.
  • Consider supplementation for specific nutrients if advised by a healthcare professional.

Hydration:

Water:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking an adequate amount of water throughout the day.
  • Adjust water intake based on factors like climate, physical activity, and individual needs.

Portion control:

Balanced Meals:

  • Practice portion control to avoid overeating.
  • Divide your plate into sections for proteins, whole grains, and vegetables to create balanced meals.

Meal timing:

Regular meals:

  • Aim for regular and consistent meal times.
  • Include snacks if needed to maintain energy levels between meals.

Limit proccesed food:

Minimize proccesed food:

  • Limit the consumption of processed and packaged foods high in sodium, added sugars, and unhealthy fats.
  • Choose whole, minimally processed foods whenever possible.

Dietery fiber:

Fiber ritch foods:

  • Include high-fiber foods like whole grains, legumes, fruits, and vegetables to support digestive health.
  • Gradually increase fiber intake to prevent digestive discomfort.

Indivisualised nutrition:

Indivisual needs:

  • Consider individual dietary needs and preferences.
  • Consult with a healthcare professional or registered dietitian for personalized advice.

Moderation:

Moderation:

  • Enjoy a wide variety of foods in moderation.
  • Be mindful of portion sizes and listen to your body's hunger and fullness cues.

If you want to loose weight

Calculate Your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE):

  • Use an online TDEE calculator or consult with a dietitian to estimate the number of calories your body needs to maintain its current weight based on your activity level.

Create a Calorie Deficit:

  • To lose weight, aim for a daily calorie intake that is below your TDEE. A common recommendation is to create a deficit of 500 to 1,000 calories per day for a gradual and sustainable weight loss of about 1-2 pounds per week.

Healthy Rate of Weight Loss:

  • Losing weight too quickly may not be sustainable or healthy. Aim for a gradual and steady weight loss to ensure you're getting essential nutrients and maintaining muscle mass.
